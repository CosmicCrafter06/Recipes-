Nihari

History

Nihari originated in the Mughal era and was traditionally eaten by royals after morning prayers. It is a slow-cooked stew made with beef or mutton, infused with aromatic spices.

Recipe

  1. Heat oil in a pot, add sliced onions, and fry until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut√© for a minute.
  3. Stir in beef/mutton, salt, and spices, then cook for 10 minutes.
  4. Add wheat flour mixed with water to thicken the gravy
  5. - Simmer for 4-6 hours on low heat.
  6. - Garnish with fried onions, green chilies, and lemon juice. Serve with naan.
