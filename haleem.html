Haleem

History

Haleem has Persian and Arabic roots and was introduced to the Indian subcontinent by Mughal emperors. It is a rich, slow-cooked dish made with wheat, lentils, and meat.

Recipe

  1. Soak wheat, barley, and lentils overnight.
  2. Cook meat with onions, ginger-garlic paste, and spices until tender.
  3. Blend the cooked grains into a thick paste.
  4. Mix the meat and grains, then simmer for an hour.
  5. Garnish with fried onions, lemon juice, and coriander. Serve hot.
